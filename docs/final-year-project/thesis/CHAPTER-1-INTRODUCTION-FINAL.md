## 1.1 Background and Context

Cambodia continues to face high traffic incident risk due to rapid urban motorization and uneven enforcement coverage. Traditional checkpoint-based policing cannot continuously monitor all critical intersections and road segments. CamTraffic addresses this enforcement gap with AI-assisted detection and workflow automation.

## 1.2 Problem Statement

Manual violation detection and paper-heavy processing create delayed response, inconsistent evidence quality, and limited traceability. Police teams need a system that can capture evidence, classify sign-related violations, and route actionable records quickly to officers and drivers.

## 1.3 Research Objectives

1. Build a practical AI pipeline for Cambodian traffic sign and plate workflows.
2. Integrate detection and OCR outputs into a full enforcement information system.
3. Validate usability for administrators, officers, and drivers.
4. Provide deployment-ready architecture for scaled production rollout.

## 1.4 Research Questions

1. Can YOLO-based models provide useful baseline quality on Cambodian traffic scenes?
2. Can OCR be integrated for practical vehicle/plate-linked violation handling?
3. Which architecture best supports reliability, observability, and role-based operations?
